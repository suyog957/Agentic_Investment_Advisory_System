# Investment Risk Profiles

## Overview
Risk tolerance determines how much volatility an investor can emotionally and financially withstand.
It is influenced by time horizon, income stability, existing assets, and personal psychology.

---

## Conservative Risk Profile

**Who it suits:** Near-retirement investors (5–10 years), low income stability, or those who cannot afford
significant portfolio drawdowns.

**Typical Allocation:**
- US Bonds: 40–50%
- International Bonds: 10–15%
- US Equities: 20–30%
- International Equities: 5–10%
- Cash / Money Market: 5–10%

**Expected annual return:** 4–6%
**Max acceptable drawdown:** ~10–15%
**Key ETFs:** BND, AGG, VGSH, VTIP, VIG

---

## Moderate Risk Profile

**Who it suits:** Mid-career investors (10–20 years to goal), stable income, comfortable with some volatility
in exchange for higher long-term growth.

**Typical Allocation:**
- US Equities: 40–50%
- International Equities: 10–15%
- US Bonds: 25–35%
- International Bonds: 5–10%
- REITs / Alternatives: 5%

**Expected annual return:** 6–8%
**Max acceptable drawdown:** ~20–25%
**Key ETFs:** VTI, VXUS, BND, BNDX, VNQ

---

## Aggressive Risk Profile

**Who it suits:** Young investors (20+ years to goal), high income stability, high risk tolerance.

**Typical Allocation:**
- US Equities: 60–70%
- International Equities: 15–20%
- Bonds: 5–15%
- REITs / Alternatives: 5–10%

**Expected annual return:** 8–10%+
**Max acceptable drawdown:** ~40–50%
**Key ETFs:** VTI, QQQ, VWO, VXUS, VNQ

---

## Risk Assessment Questions

1. If your portfolio dropped 20% in a month, would you: (a) sell immediately, (b) hold, (c) buy more?
2. What is your investment time horizon in years?
3. Do you have an emergency fund covering 6+ months of expenses?
4. Is your income stable and predictable?
5. Do you have any upcoming large expenses (home purchase, tuition) in the next 5 years?

---

## Age-Based Rules of Thumb

- **110 minus age rule:** equity allocation = 110 - age (e.g., age 42 → ~68% equities)
- **120 minus age rule (more aggressive):** equity allocation = 120 - age
- These are starting points only; actual allocation should be calibrated to personal goals and risk tolerance.

---

## Dual-Goal Allocation Adjustment

When an investor has both a long-term goal (retirement, 20+ years) and a medium-term goal
(education, 7–10 years), a split portfolio approach is recommended:

- **Bucket 1 (Education, 7–10 years):** Moderate-Conservative → 40–50% equities, 50–60% bonds
- **Bucket 2 (Retirement, 20+ years):** Moderate-Aggressive → 65–75% equities, 25–35% bonds

This prevents raiding long-term retirement funds for medium-term needs.

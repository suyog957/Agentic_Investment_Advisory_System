# Core Investing Principles

## 1. Start with Financial Foundations

Before investing, ensure:
- **Emergency fund:** 3–6 months of living expenses in cash/HYSA.
- **High-interest debt paid off:** Credit cards (>15% APR) should be cleared first.
- **Tax-advantaged accounts maxed first:**
  - 401(k) up to employer match (free money).
  - Max 401(k) ($23,000/year in 2024).
  - Max IRA ($7,000/year in 2024; $7,500 if age 50+).
  - Max HSA if eligible ($4,150 single / $8,300 family in 2024).
  - Then invest in taxable brokerage accounts.

---

## 2. Time in Market > Timing the Market

- No one consistently predicts market tops and bottoms.
- Missing the 10 best trading days in a 20-year period can cut returns nearly in half.
- **Buy and hold:** Staying invested through volatility outperforms most active strategies.
- Historical US equity average return: ~10% nominal, ~7% real (inflation-adjusted) per year.

---

## 3. Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA)

- Invest a fixed amount at regular intervals (e.g., monthly) regardless of market conditions.
- Naturally buys more shares when prices are low, fewer when prices are high.
- Removes emotional decision-making from the process.
- Ideal for systematic investing from paychecks or cash savings.

---

## 4. Compound Growth

The most powerful force in investing. Key variables:
- **Principal:** Starting amount invested.
- **Rate of return:** Average annual return (use conservative 6–7% real for planning).
- **Time:** The most critical factor. Starting early dramatically increases outcomes.

**Example:** $100,000 invested at 7% annual return:
- After 10 years: ~$197,000
- After 20 years: ~$387,000
- After 30 years: ~$761,000

---

## 5. Diversification

"Don't put all your eggs in one basket."
- Across asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate).
- Across geographies (US + international).
- Across sectors (tech, healthcare, consumer, energy).
- No single holding should dominate the portfolio (>10% concentration risk).

---

## 6. Keep Costs Low

Every dollar paid in fees is a dollar not compounding.

- 1% fee on $350K portfolio over 20 years at 7% return costs ~$175,000 in foregone wealth.
- Prioritize low-cost index ETFs (< 0.10% expense ratio).
- Minimize transaction costs by holding long-term.
- Avoid frequent trading in taxable accounts.

---

## 7. Tax Efficiency

**Tax-Advantaged Account Priority:**
1. 401(k) / 403(b) — pre-tax or Roth
2. IRA — traditional or Roth
3. HSA — triple tax advantage
4. 529 Plan — for education expenses (tax-free growth for qualified expenses)
5. Taxable brokerage — last resort

**Tax-Efficient Asset Location:**
- Hold bonds and REITs in tax-advantaged accounts (high income = high taxes).
- Hold equities in taxable accounts (qualified dividends + LTCG taxed at lower rates).

**Tax-Loss Harvesting:**
- Sell losing positions in taxable accounts to realize losses.
- Use losses to offset capital gains or up to $3,000 of ordinary income per year.
- Immediately reinvest in a similar (not identical) fund to maintain market exposure.

---

## 8. Avoid Behavioral Biases

| Bias | Description | Counter-strategy |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| Loss aversion | Fear of losses > desire for gains | Automate investments; don't check daily |
| Recency bias | Overweighting recent events | Stick to long-term plan |
| Herding | Following the crowd into hot investments | Rebalance contrarily |
| Overconfidence | Believing you can beat the market | Use passive index funds |
| Anchoring | Fixating on purchase price | Focus on intrinsic value and plan |

---

## 9. Education Fund (529 Plan)

- Contributions grow tax-free when used for qualified education expenses.
- Many states offer a state income tax deduction on contributions.
- Can be used for tuition, room and board, books, computers.
- Unused funds can be rolled to a Roth IRA (up to $35,000 lifetime, per SECURE 2.0 Act).
- Recommended strategy: Start early; aggressive allocation when child is young, shift conservative as college approaches.

---

## 10. Retirement Planning Principles

- **Rule of 25:** You need ~25x your annual expenses at retirement to sustain a 4% withdrawal rate.
- **4% Safe Withdrawal Rate (SWR):** Historically safe for 30-year retirement periods.
- **Social Security:** Plan around it but don't depend solely on it; delaying to 70 increases benefit ~8%/year.
- **Sequence of Returns Risk:** Bad returns early in retirement can devastate a portfolio; de-risk 5 years before retirement.
- **Healthcare costs:** Major wildcard; consider HSA accumulation and long-term care insurance planning.

---

## 11. Rebalancing Discipline

- Set target allocation and rebalance annually (or when drifted >5%).
- Rebalancing forces buying low and selling high systematically.
- Prevents portfolio from drifting into unintended risk levels after a bull market.

---

## 12. Investment Policy Statement (IPS)

Document your investment strategy including:
- Goals and time horizons
- Target allocation
- Rebalancing rules
- Contribution plan
- What circumstances would trigger a strategy change

This prevents emotional, reactive decisions during market turmoil.

# ETF Selection Guide

## Why ETFs?

Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs) provide:
- **Diversification:** Instant exposure to hundreds or thousands of securities.
- **Low cost:** Expense ratios typically 0.03%–0.20% vs 0.5–1.5% for active funds.
- **Tax efficiency:** Lower capital gains distributions than mutual funds.
- **Liquidity:** Trade throughout the day like stocks.
- **Transparency:** Holdings disclosed daily.

## ETF Selection Criteria

1. **Expense Ratio:** Prefer < 0.15%; avoid anything > 0.5% without strong justification.
2. **Assets Under Management (AUM):** Prefer > $1B for liquidity.
3. **Tracking Error:** Lower is better; fund should closely mirror its index.
4. **Issuer:** Vanguard, iShares (BlackRock), and Schwab are most cost-competitive.
5. **Tax Efficiency:** ETFs generally better than mutual funds in taxable accounts.

---

## What to Avoid

- **Leveraged ETFs** (e.g., TQQQ): Not suitable for long-term investing; decay over time.
- **Inverse ETFs:** Designed for short-term trading, not buy-and-hold.
- **Thematic/Niche ETFs:** High expense ratios, concentrated risk, often trend-following.
- **Cryptocurrency ETFs:** High volatility, speculative — exclude per client constraints.
- **High-expense active ETFs:** Rarely outperform passive equivalents net of fees.

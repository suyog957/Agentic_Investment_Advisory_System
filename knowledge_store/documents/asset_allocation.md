# Asset Allocation Strategies

## What is Asset Allocation?

Asset allocation is the process of dividing an investment portfolio among different asset categories
(equities, fixed income, cash, real estate, commodities) to balance risk and return according to an
investor's goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon.

---

## Major Asset Classes

### Equities (Stocks)
- **US Large Cap:** Highest liquidity, well-researched, lower volatility within equities.
- **US Small/Mid Cap:** Higher growth potential, higher volatility.
- **International Developed:** Europe, Japan, Australia — diversification, some currency risk.
- **Emerging Markets:** Higher growth potential, higher political/currency risk.

### Fixed Income (Bonds)
- **US Treasury Bonds:** Safest, backed by US government. Low yield.
- **Corporate Bonds (Investment Grade):** Slightly higher yield with manageable credit risk.
- **High-Yield Bonds:** Significant credit risk; not recommended for conservative profiles.
- **TIPS (Inflation-Protected):** Treasury bonds that adjust for inflation — useful in inflationary periods.
- **Municipal Bonds:** Tax-exempt; beneficial for high-income investors.

### Real Estate (REITs)
- REITs provide exposure to real estate without direct ownership.
- They typically offer dividend income and moderate capital appreciation.
- Recommended allocation: 5–10% of total portfolio.

### Cash & Equivalents
- Money market funds, short-term treasuries.
- Preserve capital and provide liquidity.
- Keep only what is needed for short-term needs + emergency fund.

---

## Strategic Asset Allocation (SAA)

SAA sets a long-term target allocation and rebalances back to it periodically.

**Example for Moderate Investor (age 42, 20-year horizon):**
- US Equities: 45%
- International Equities: 15%
- US Bonds: 25%
- International Bonds: 5%
- REITs: 5%
- Cash: 5%

---

## Glide Path Strategy

Used for goal-based investing: automatically shift allocation toward bonds as the goal date approaches.

**Education Fund Glide Path (7–10 years away):**
- Year 0–4: 50% equities / 50% bonds
- Year 5–7: 30% equities / 70% bonds
- Year 8+: 10% equities / 90% bonds

**Retirement Glide Path:**
- 20+ years out: 70–80% equities
- 10–20 years out: 60–70% equities
- 5–10 years out: 50% equities
- At retirement: 40–50% equities (still need 20–30 more years of growth)

---

## Rebalancing

### When to Rebalance
- **Calendar rebalancing:** Annually or semi-annually.
- **Threshold rebalancing:** When any asset class drifts more than 5% from target.
- **Combination:** Annual review with threshold trigger.

### Tax Considerations
- Rebalance inside tax-advantaged accounts (401k, IRA) first to avoid taxable events.
- In taxable accounts, use new contributions to buy underweight assets.
- Tax-loss harvesting: sell losers in taxable accounts to offset gains.

---

## Diversification Principles

1. **Within asset classes:** Don't hold a single stock or bond; use funds/ETFs.
2. **Across asset classes:** Equities + bonds + real estate reduce overall portfolio volatility.
3. **Geographically:** US + international exposure hedges country-specific risks.
4. **By factor:** Growth + value + dividend exposure.
5. **By time:** Dollar-cost averaging spreads market timing risk.
